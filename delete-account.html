Ordinary Archive Account and Data Deletion

Effective date: June 23, 2026.

Ordinary Archive uses your Google Account to sign in on Android and to support Google Play purchases. Ordinary Archive does not create a separate password-based account on its own servers in this build.

Delete Local Journal Data

You can delete journal entries, media, settings, and locally stored journal data from inside the app. Open Ordinary Archive, go to Settings, and use the delete/reset controls for journal entries or all local app data.

Delete Cloud Sync Data

If you enabled optional encrypted cloud sync, delete the encrypted Ordinary Archive archive bundle from the cloud provider you connected, such as Google Drive, Microsoft OneDrive, Apple iCloud/CloudKit, or your configured Amazon-backed storage. Ordinary Archive uploads encrypted archive bundles only when cloud sync is enabled by the user.

Delete Account Association

To remove the app's association with your Google Account, open your Google Account security settings and remove Ordinary Archive from third-party access. You can also uninstall the app to remove local app access from the device.

Request Help

If you need help deleting app-associated data, email support@ordinaryarchive.app with the subject Delete Ordinary Archive Data. Do not include journal content in the email. We will only use your email to respond to the deletion request.

Data Retention

In this build, journal content is local-first and is not stored on Ordinary Archive developer servers. Data stored by Google Play, Google Account, cloud storage providers, or app stores is controlled by those providers and their account controls. Support request emails may be retained for ordinary business records and abuse prevention.
